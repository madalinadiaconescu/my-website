Rule 10 - Be precise in your speech

As per my understanding

Vague words hide vague thinking. Naming the actual problem: “I'm procrastinating on the report because I'm afraid of the review”, gives me somewhere to push. Precision also makes me kinder, it prevents me from blaming everything and everyone at once. When I say exactly what I mean, plans become possible and conflicts become solvable.

Key takeaways

  • Name the real problem, vagueness hides fear.
  • Specific requests and definitions make plans possible.
  • Precision reduces blame and makes feedback useful.
