Rule 10 - Be precise in your speech

As per my understanding

Vague words hide vague thinking. When I name the actual problem: “I’m avoiding the report because I’m scared of the review”, I finally have somewhere to push.

Precision makes me kinder and more effective. Specific requests and definitions turn conflicts into solvable tasks.

Key takeaways

  • Name the real problem, vagueness hides fear.
  • Specific requests and definitions make plans possible.
  • Precision reduces blame and makes feedback useful.
