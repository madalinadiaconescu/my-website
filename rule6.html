Rule 6 - Set your house in perfect order before you criticize the world

As per my understanding

Before I try to fix the world, I can fix my corner of it: my room, my calendar, my code, my habits. Order isn't perfection; it's honesty about what I've been avoiding and the courage to handle it piece by piece.

When I clean up two square meters of chaos, I prove to myself I'm not helpless. From that base, my opinions carry weight because I've done the work that backs them up.

Key takeaways

  • Start where my hands are: room, desk, calendar, code.
  • Clean one avoided corner a day; momentum follows.
  • Order creates credibility; opinions carry weight after action.
