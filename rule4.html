Rule 4 - Compare yourself to who you were yesterday, not to who someone else is today

As per my understanding

Scrolling is a trap: I see end results, not the messy middle. This rule shrinks my world to something I can control: yesterday vs. today. Did I write the paragraph, fix the bug, go to the gym, call my mom? Progress becomes measurable and honest. Envy fades when I run my own race. I can be a fraction better daily and let compounding do its quiet magic.

Key takeaways

  • Run my own race: measure today vs. yesterday.
  • Track tiny, visible wins and let them compound.
  • Limit comparison loops (feeds) that distort reality.
