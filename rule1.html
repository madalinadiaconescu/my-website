Rule 1 - Stand up straight with your shoulders back

As per my understanding

I learned that posture isn't just about looks: it's a decision about how I meet the world. When I carry myself upright, I'm telling my nervous system I'm capable of handling what's coming. It changes how people respond to me and how I respond to myself. The rule isn't about pretending to be fearless, it's about choosing responsibility over shrinking. When I lift my shoulders, I also lift the weight I'm meant to carry.

Key takeaways

  • Posture is a micro-commitment to responsibility.
  • Body language updates mood and momentum.
  • Stand up first, then act like the person who can handle what follows.
